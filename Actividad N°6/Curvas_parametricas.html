Curvas paramétricas en computación gráfica

Desarrollo conceptual, importancia, tipos, usos en computación gráfica, ejemplo gráfico y bibliografía.

Concepto

Las curvas paramétricas son una forma de representación matemática en la que cada punto of the curve is obtained from one or more functions that depend on a parameter, usually t. Formally, a curve can be seen as a mapping from an interval of parameters to the plane or space, which allows expressing points of the form P(t) and describing smooth trajectories in both 2D and 3D.

In computer graphics, this representation is especially important because it does not only work with static figures, but with geometric entities that must be edited, interpolated, animated, and finally visualized. Parametric curves allow modeling continuous geometry with few control data, which improves design accuracy and reduces storage complexity compared to a purely discrete representation.

Also, parameterization separates the geometric shape from the way the curve is traversed. This means that the parameter can also be interpreted as time in animation, allowing for describing smooth controlled trajectories of movement, camera paths, and virtual object displacements.

Importance

Parametric curves are important because they constitute a foundation for smooth geometric modeling, digital typography, vector illustration, animation by trajectories, and the construction of more complex surfaces.

Where they are used

They are used in vector design software, CAD systems, font editing, 2D and 3D animation, camera paths, and contour modeling. They also appear in tessellation processes, because a mathematical curve must be discretized into polylines before being rasterized.

Value in graphics

Their practical value is that they offer continuity, shape control, and ease of evaluation. This allows refining, subdividing, or approximating a curve according to rendering needs.

Main types

Among the most important families are Bézier, Hermite, B-spline, and NURBS curves. Bézier curves are widely used because they are defined with few control points and possess very useful geometric properties. B-splines add more local control, so that modifying a control point does not alter the entire curve, but only a specific region.

  • Bézier: widely used in vector graphics, modeling, and animation.
  • Hermite: suitable when interested in controlling position and tangency at the ends.
  • B-spline: allow smooth continuity between segments and better local control.
  • NURBS: very used in CAD for its high geometric precision.

Uses in computer graphics

In computer graphics, parametric curves serve to construct animation trajectories, object contours, extrusion profiles, camera paths, interpolation between states, and surface generation.

An important technical aspect is tessellation. Graphics hardware finally draws segments and triangles, not abstract mathematical entities; therefore, parametric curves are converted into polylines by parameter sampling.
